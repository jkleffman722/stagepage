# Venue Tech Packet — Research Reference

> **Purpose:** Define every field a venue tech packet should contain so StagePage can audit and improve the current venue-side build, and ensure the packet is comprehensive enough to satisfy a real touring PM's advancing needs.

---

## What a Venue Tech Packet Is

A venue tech packet is the comprehensive document a venue sends to a touring production manager during the advancing process — typically 2–4 weeks before a show. It tells the tour everything they need to know about what the venue can and can't provide.

A well-built tech packet dramatically reduces back-and-forth emails, prevents show-day surprises, and signals to touring PMs that the venue is professional. A vague or incomplete packet generates 20+ clarification emails and erodes trust before the crew even arrives.

**Key principle:** Specificity wins. "We have a great PA" is useless. "L-Acoustics K2, 12 boxes per side, flown on permanent points" is what a PM needs.

## What Touring PMs Say Is Most Often Missing

These are the fields that generate the most clarification emails — things PMs constantly have to ask about because venues leave them out:

| Missing Field | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Snake route length (stage to FOH) | PM must size their cable runs before arriving |
| Available amperage at load point (not service size) | Service size ≠ available capacity |
| Per-rigging-point weight limit | Not just "we have rigging" |
| FOH position: fixed or movable? | Critical for large productions that bring their own mix position |
| Number of *working* IEM receivers | Venues list what they own, not what's functional |
| Fixture inventory with counts | "Full lighting rig" is meaningless |
| Monitor mix count | Often lists console model but not how many aux sends are routed |
| Dock height in inches | Huge variance; directly affects whether a truck can dock |
| Overnight crew access | Can production stay and work overnight? |
| Wireless frequency bands | Needed weeks out for RF coordination |
| Backup contacts | One contact is insufficient; that person may be unavailable |
| Known equipment issues | Venues never volunteer this; it always surfaces at the worst time |
| Distance from panels to stage | Determines extension cable needs |
| Emergency exit locations | Safety requirement; often missing from packet |

## How Tech Packets Differ by Venue Size

| | Club (50–300) | Theater / Ballroom (300–2,000) | Arena / PAC (2,000+) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Length** | 1–2 pages | 5–15 pages | 15–50+ pages |
| **PA** | Fixed house system, likely modest | Proper system, some flexibility | Touring-grade, extensive specs |
| **Console** | Often analog, limited channels | Digital, 32–48 ch typical | Flagship digital, full I/O |
| **Monitor mixes** | 2–4 | 4–8 | 8–12+ |
| **Rigging** | None or minimal | Some counterweight rigging | Full rigging infrastructure |
| **Power** | Standard outlets, limited amperage | 50–200A available to production | 200A–400A+ |
| **Lighting** | Basic, minimal fixture inventory | Moderate rig with some moving lights | Full rig, hundreds of fixtures |
| **Hospitality** | Minimal | Moderate | Comprehensive |
| **Staff** | 1–2 contacts | 3–5 contacts | Department-level contacts |
| **Diagrams** | Rarely | Sometimes | Always |
| **Format** | Usually email or simple PDF | PDF with sections | Comprehensive binder/portal |

## Format & Delivery Best Practices

**Preferred format:** PDF (single document, printable, shareable)
- Contact info in the footer or header of every page
- Version number and date on every page
- Table of contents for documents over 10 pages
- Diagrams with clear labels and scale
- Black-and-white printable (no color-dependent information)
- Legible at 3 AM — large type, clear section breaks, no walls of text

**Delivery timeline:**
- Available on request at booking time
- Send proactively 4–6 weeks before show
- PM should receive in time to review before their first advance call

**Emerging best practice:** Online/live version (not a static PDF) so updates propagate automatically without needing to re-email.

## Audit: What Our Current Build Covers vs. What's Missing

Use this to identify gaps in StagePage's existing venue packet builder. Fields to check:

**Audio:** PA system, FOH console, monitor system, IEM specs, mic/DI inventory, snake specs, channel count
**Lighting:** Console, fixture inventory by type and count, truss positions with load capacities
**Stage:** Dimensions, surface type, wing space, ceiling/grid height, rigging specs with weight limits
**Power:** Panel locations, available amperage, outlet types and locations, phase config, distance to stage
**Load-in:** Dock dimensions and height, number of bays, forklift, elevator, parking for trucks/buses/crew
**Hospitality:** Green rooms (count, capacity, amenities), showers, catering, WiFi, curfew
**Schedule:** Crew access time, load-in window, soundcheck window, doors, hard curfew, load-out
**Contacts:** Role-specific contacts (audio, lighting, stage, rigging, general), backup contacts, emergency
**Union:** Affiliation, mandatory crew requirements, overtime rules
**Backline:** What the venue owns or can source locally
